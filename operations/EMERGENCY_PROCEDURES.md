# Emergency Procedures

This guide outlines the emergency response steps for Ekubo Protocol EVM deployments.

---

## 1. Detection

- Monitor for critical issues (e.g., exploits, major bugs, abnormal activity).
- Use automated alerts and community reports.

---

## 2. Initial Response

- Confirm the validity and severity of the incident.
- Notify core contributors and DAO governance immediately.

---

## 3. Containment

- Pause affected contracts if possible (using emergency pause or admin controls).
- Disable vulnerable features to prevent further impact.
- Communicate status to users and stakeholders.

---

## 4. Investigation

- Analyze the root cause and scope of the incident.
- Gather logs, transaction data, and relevant evidence.

---

## 5. Remediation

- Develop and test a fix or mitigation.
- Deploy emergency patches as needed.
- Coordinate with auditors or external experts if required.

---

## 6. Recovery

- Restore normal operations and verify system integrity.
- Monitor for further issues and confirm resolution.

---

## 7. Communication

- Provide regular updates to the community and DAO.
- Issue a post-mortem report after resolution.

---

## 8. Documentation

- Record all actions, decisions, and outcomes in the audit log.
- Update incident response and emergency procedures as needed.
