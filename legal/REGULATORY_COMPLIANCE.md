## Regulatory Compliance Considerations

This document outlines key regulatory considerations for users and operators of the Ekubo Protocol EVM implementation.

---

### 1. Jurisdictional Compliance

- Users and operators must comply with all applicable laws and regulations in their jurisdiction.
- This may include financial, securities, anti-money laundering (AML), and tax regulations.

### 2. Financial Regulations

- The protocol may be subject to financial regulations depending on its use and geographic location.
- Operators should consult legal counsel to determine if registration, licensing, or reporting is required.

### 3. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & Know Your Customer (KYC)

- Ekubo Protocol EVM does not perform AML or KYC checks by default.
- Operators are responsible for implementing AML/KYC procedures if required by law.

### 4. Tax Compliance

- Revenue generated through the protocol may be subject to taxation.
- Users and operators are responsible for reporting and paying any applicable taxes.

### 5. Data Protection

- The protocol does not collect personal data by default.
- Operators must ensure compliance with data protection laws (e.g., GDPR) if personal data is processed.

### 6. Third-Party Services

- Interactions with third-party services (e.g., exchanges, RPC providers) may introduce additional regulatory requirements.
- Review and comply with the policies of all integrated services.

### 7. Updates & Changes

- Regulatory requirements may change over time.
- Operators and users should stay informed and update practices as needed.

### 8. Disclaimer

- This document does not constitute legal advice.
- Consult with qualified legal professionals for guidance on regulatory compliance.
