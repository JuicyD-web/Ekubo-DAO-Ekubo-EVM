# Treasury Management

This document describes how the Ekubo Protocol EVM treasury is managed, including fund allocation, oversight, and reporting.

---

## 1. Treasury Structure

- The protocol treasury holds funds collected from protocol fees, earnings, and other revenue streams.
- Treasury assets may include ETH, stablecoins (USDC, DAI), and other supported tokens.

## 2. Fund Allocation

- Funds are allocated for:
  - Revenue sharing payments to Ekubo DAO
  - Liquidity incentives and rewards
  - Development and maintenance expenses
  - Security audits and compliance
  - Governance and community initiatives

## 3. Oversight & Governance

- Treasury management is overseen by Ekubo DAO governance.
- All major expenditures and allocations require DAO proposal and approval.
- Treasury transactions are recorded on-chain for transparency.

## 4. Reporting

- Regular financial reports are published, detailing treasury balances, inflows, outflows, and allocations.
- Reports are archived in the financial documentation directory.

## 5. Security

- Treasury funds are secured using multi-signature wallets or DAO-controlled smart contracts.
- Security practices are reviewed periodically to mitigate risks.

## 6. Updates

- Treasury management policies may be updated by DAO governance.
- Changes are documented and communicated to stakeholders.
